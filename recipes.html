Recipes

My approach to meal planning is to embrace the cooking aspect as much as possible, experiment with different techniques. You'll surpise yourself how good a cook you actually can be. I'm never going to force you to eat anything you don't like or enjoy, as that is not sustainable and you'll just revert back to older eating habits

Upon signing up to my 'Full time Consultation', youll gain access to endless amounts of easy to follow recipes that fit into your daily nutrional target. I am also available to create personal recipes upon request

Eating healthy doesn't have to be boring, you can experiment with everyday spices to create exciting meals

See below for an example of a full day's meal plan. This plan is both tasty and nutritious as it includes a good comination of the macronutrients: protein, carbohydrates and fats.

Breakfast

Blueberry Protein Pancakes

Ingredients
  • 50g oats
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp of honey
  • 1 bannana (peeled)
  • 10ml low fat milk
  • 1/2 tsp of olive oil
  • 3 tsb of blueberries to serve on the side
  • 1 dallop of 0% fat greek yoghurt
Method
  1. Blend the oats eggs, honey, bananas and milk. (if you don't have a processor/blender thats fine, they won''t win any presentation awards but they'll taste as good
  2. Combine the pancake mixture with some blueberries and stir
  3. Lightly oil the pan, and heat to a medium heat
  4. Dallop the pancakes mix using a ladle into even portions on the pan. Cook till brown on each side
  5. Serve with 0%fat greek yoghurt and more blueberries.

Lunch

Chicken Madras

Ingredients
  • 125g cooked rice
  • 150g chicken
  • 1/2 tin of chopped tomatoes
  • Thumb sized grated ginger
  • Clove of garlic
  • Red chilli
  • Tsp of olive oil
  • Tsp each of turmeric, chilli powder, cumin and coriander
Method
  1. Blitz the garlic, onion, red chilli and ginger in a blender to create a paste.
  2. Heat pan with olive oil and add paste to pan. Cook for 2 mins.

Dinner

Spaghetti Meatballs

Ingredients
  • 125g lean beef mince
  • 15g of breadcrumbs
  • Handful chopped fresh basil
  • 1 chopped onion
  • 200ml of tomato passasta
  • grated carrot
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • tsp each of peppper, salt, paprika and oregano
  • tbsp of paresen cheese to serve
  • 90g of Spaghetti
Method
  1. In a large bowl mix the mince, breadcrumbs, basil and spices. Roll into even sized meatballs
  2. Heat a saucepan to medium heat with a tsp of olive oil. Add the chopped onion and garlic and cook for 2 mins.
  3. Add the meatballs and cook for 5 mins until browned on each side.
  4. Add the tomato passata, reduce the heat and simmer for 20mins. (Stir occassionally)
  5. Boil the spaghetti. Follow the instructions on the pack
  6. Serve the meatballs and tomato sauce on a bed of cooked spaghetti and top with parmesan cheese
